How should the Query Editor's UX evolve?

Three directions for the SQL editor + its controls (Format, Database, Splitting, Rollups) and the rollup status pills. Click the one whose direction you like — or mix & match in the terminal.

A · Unified toolbar

All controls in one compact toolbar above the editor. One adaptive status line that morphs from the pre-run prediction into the post-run result. Macros help moves behind a ? popover. Best for density & a single scan line.

B · Grafana-native

Rebuild on Grafana's own EditorRows/EditorField + Badge components. Theme-tokened (correct in light & dark), native status Badge, help as an ⓘ icon. Most idiomatic & lowest long-term maintenance; controls sit under the editor like other Grafana datasources.

C · Minimal / editor-first

Maximize the editor. Everything secondary collapses into one slim bottom bar: live rollup status on the left, an Options disclosure + help on the right. Fewest pixels spent on chrome; controls one click away.

All three keep the existing behavior (Cmd+Enter to run, autocomplete, resize, source-fallback). The difference is layout, grouping, and how the rollup status reads. Pick a direction and I'll detail it; theming/accessibility fixes come with whichever you choose.
